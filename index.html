PointGAA: Learning Feature-Conditioned Pseudo-Coordinates for Point Cloud Attention

Overview

Attention mechanisms have achieved remarkable success across various domains, yet positional information is often treated as auxiliary. For point clouds, local geometric relationships are crucial for guiding attention. We propose PointGAA, a Geo-Augmented Attention module that learns feature-conditioned pseudo-coordinates and integrates them with projected relative coordinates, allowing attention to adaptively fuse semantic and geometric cues. Additionally, a Neighborhood Context Aggregator captures local context and injects it into attention logits, enhancing discriminability. Extensive experiments on ScanObjectNN (classification), ShapeNetPart (part segmentation), and S3DIS (semantic segmentation) demonstrate that PointGAA consistently improves accuracy and robustness over strong baselines.

Motivation

To better quantify the relative importance of semantic features and positional cues for attention, we conducted a study on the ModelNet40\cite{Modelnet40} classification benchmark. When attention coefficients are computed solely from feature similarity, the model achieves a classification accuracy of $92.0\%$, whereas using only positional information yields a higher accuracy of $93.3\%$. We further experimented with a learnable linear weighting that combines feature-driven and position-driven attention logits. After training, the learned weight assigns greater importance to the position-based term than to the feature-based term, as illustrated in Fig.~\ref{fig:2}. This result indicates that, for point clouds, positional cues play a more significant role in determining attention coefficients than semantic features—a characteristic not shared by image or text data, where local features vary but pixel or token positions remain constant. This observation indicates that geometric cues can provide more discriminative signals than feature similarity alone for neighborhood selection in point clouds. Motivated by this finding, we propose PointGAA, which learns feature-conditioned pseudo-coordinates and adaptively fuses them with raw relative positions to enable a tighter, data-driven integration of geometry and semantics in attention computation.

Method Overview

Overall architecture of PointGAA. We adopt a U-Net–style encoder–decoder architecture to support both classification and segmentation tasks. In the encoder, a Feature Transformation module first performs dimensionality reduction or expansion of point features, followed by downsampling via farthest point sampling. Next, the Spatial Encoding module encodes positional information for each point and fuses it with feature descriptors, thereby enriching local representations. Crucially, at every Local Aggregation stage in the encoder, our proposed PointGAA module is applied to jointly model semantic and geometric information, enhancing attention-based feature aggregation. The decoder progressively restores spatial resolution using nearest-neighbor interpolation and feature propagation. Finally, task-specific heads are employed for segmentation and classification.

Experiments

We evaluate our model on five widely-used point cloud benchmarks spanning three representative tasks. For shape classification, we use ModelNet40 and ScanObjectNN; for part segmentation, ShapeNetPart; and for indoor scene semantic segmentation, S3DIS.
